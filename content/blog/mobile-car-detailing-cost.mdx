# How Much Does Mobile Car Detailing Cost in 2026?

Mobile car detailing costs between **$50 and $350** for most vehicles in 2026, depending on the service level, vehicle size, and your location. A basic exterior wash and interior wipe-down typically runs $50 to $100, while a full interior and exterior detail with clay bar treatment, polish, and leather conditioning will set you back $150 to $350. Premium services like ceramic coating application or multi-stage paint correction can push prices well above $500.

These numbers reflect real pricing data from detailers listed across the country. If you are seeing quotes significantly outside these ranges, it is worth understanding why — and this guide will help you do exactly that.

## How Location Affects Pricing

Where you live has a significant impact on what you will pay. Detailers in major metros have higher overhead — insurance, fuel, and cost of living all factor into their rates. Here is how pricing breaks down by city tier for a full interior and exterior detail on a midsize sedan:

| City Tier | Example Cities | Avg. Full Detail Price |
|---|---|---|
| Major Metro | New York, Los Angeles, San Francisco, Chicago | $225 – $375 |
| Mid-Size City | Austin, Nashville, Denver, Charlotte | $175 – $300 |
| Small City / Rural | Boise, Knoxville, Tulsa, Spokane | $125 – $225 |

The biggest cost driver in expensive markets is not greed — it is the cost of doing business. A detailer operating a van in Manhattan pays dramatically more for insurance, parking, and fuel than one in Tulsa. That said, competition in larger markets can sometimes push prices down for basic services.

## Vehicle Size Pricing Differences

Most mobile detailers price by vehicle size, and for good reason. A three-row SUV takes significantly more time and product than a compact sedan. Expect the following general pricing tiers:

| Vehicle Type | Price Multiplier (vs. Sedan) | Example Full Detail Range |
|---|---|---|
| Compact / Sedan | Baseline | $150 – $275 |
| Midsize SUV / Crossover | +15% to +25% | $175 – $325 |
| Full-Size SUV / Minivan | +25% to +40% | $200 – $375 |
| Full-Size Truck (Crew Cab) | +25% to +45% | $200 – $400 |
| Oversized (Sprinter Van, RV) | +50% to +100%+ | $300 – $600+ |

If a detailer quotes you a flat rate without asking about your vehicle, that is a yellow flag. Reputable operators always account for vehicle size.

## What Drives Price Differences Between Detailers?

You might get quotes of $120 and $280 for what sounds like the same service. Here is what typically accounts for the gap:

- **Product quality.** A detailer using professional-grade ceramic spray sealant and pH-balanced shampoo has higher material costs than one using off-the-shelf products from a big-box store.
- **Experience and training.** Certified detailers or those with IDA (International Detailing Association) credentials tend to charge more — and their work usually justifies it.
- **Insurance and licensing.** Fully insured operators carry higher overhead, but they also protect you if something goes wrong.
- **Equipment.** Professional-grade polishers, steam cleaners, extractors, and water filtration systems are not cheap to buy or maintain.
- **Time on site.** A thorough full detail takes 3 to 5 hours. If someone is promising a "full detail" in 45 minutes, you are not getting the same service.

## Tips for Getting the Best Value

Do not default to the cheapest quote. Instead, focus on value:

- **Book package deals.** Many detailers offer discounts when you bundle interior and exterior services or commit to recurring appointments.
- **Schedule recurring maintenance.** Monthly or biweekly maintenance washes ($50 to $80) keep your car in good shape and reduce the need for expensive deep details.
- **Ask about new customer specials.** Many detailers listed on directories like [YOURDOMAIN].com offer introductory pricing to earn your first review.
- **Avoid unnecessary add-ons.** Not every car needs paint correction or ceramic coating. A good detailer will be honest about what your vehicle actually needs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is mobile detailing more expensive than going to a shop?

Mobile detailing typically costs 10% to 25% more than a comparable shop-based service. You are paying a premium for the convenience of at-home service, plus the detailer's travel time and fuel costs. However, many people find the time savings and convenience more than worth the price difference. You also avoid the risk of lot damage at busy shops.

### How much should I tip a mobile detailer?

Tipping is not required but is appreciated, especially for exceptional work. A tip of 15% to 20% is standard if you are happy with the service. For a $200 detail, that would be $30 to $40. If the detailer is the business owner, tipping is less expected but still welcomed. Some clients offer cold drinks or snacks instead, which detailers genuinely appreciate during long summer appointments.

### How often should I get my car detailed?

For most drivers, a full detail every 4 to 6 months is sufficient, with maintenance washes every 2 to 4 weeks in between. If you have kids, pets, or a long commute, you may benefit from quarterly full details. Vehicles with ceramic coatings need less frequent deep cleaning but still benefit from regular maintenance washes to keep the coating performing at its best.

### Why do some detailers charge so much more than others?

Price variation usually comes down to product quality, experience, insurance coverage, and the time they spend on each vehicle. A detailer charging $300 for a full detail is likely spending 4+ hours on your car with professional-grade products and equipment. A $99 "full detail" usually means a quick wash with basic products and limited attention to detail. You generally get what you pay for in this industry.

### Can I negotiate detailing prices?

Most professional detailers set their prices based on calculated costs and do not negotiate on individual services. However, you can often get better rates by booking recurring service packages, referring friends, or scheduling during slower weekdays. Some detailers also offer loyalty discounts after your first few appointments. The best approach is to ask about available packages rather than trying to haggle on a quoted price.
